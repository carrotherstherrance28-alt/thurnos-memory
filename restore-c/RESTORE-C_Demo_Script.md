## Section 1 — Opening (2 minutes)

**Goal:** Make Marvin feel seen. Name the exact pain before showing any solution.

---

"Marvin, Kevin — before I show you anything, let me just say back what I'm hearing and you tell me if I'm off.

After a storm rolls through — a hail event hits two or three neighborhoods — your phone starts ringing. Maybe 2 to 5 people reach out over the next few days. You're already busy coordinating crews, tracking materials, and somehow you're also the guy trying to call these leads back.

If someone doesn't hear back within the first hour or two, they've already called the next roofer on Google. You lose the job before you ever had a conversation.

On top of that, your website got compromised on GoDaddy — so right now if someone goes looking for you online, there's friction. Even the leads who find you have a bad first impression.

What I built solves the most expensive part of that problem first. Not a full website overhaul — that comes later. What I built is your **Storm Lead Conversion System**. Its only job is to make sure that when a homeowner raises their hand after a storm, you know about it within seconds and nothing falls through the cracks.

Let me show you exactly how it works."

**Goal:** Create the magic moment — Marvin sees the email arrive in real time.

---

"This is the page a homeowner lands on when they need a roof inspection. Clean, simple — one job: get them to fill out the form."

*[Pause 3-4 seconds — let them look]*

"I'm going to play the role of a homeowner. Let's say a storm came through their neighborhood last night."

"Name — John Homeowner. Phone — test number. Address — 123 Elm Street, Indianapolis. Damage type — hail damage."

"Marvin, watch your email."

"Name, phone number, address, damage type — everything you need to call them back right now. No hunting through texts. No digging through spam. It's here, formatted, ready to act on.

"Same time — I get a Slack alert so I can monitor the system is working. You don't have to worry about the backend. That's my job."
